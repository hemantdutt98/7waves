How to Recover from Burnout?



Ananya K

Content Team, 7 Waves Life Solutions

July 5, 2022    10 min read

“If you feel burnout setting in, if you feel demoralised and exhausted, it is best for the sake of everyone, to withdraw and restore yourself.”
~Dalai Lama

Tiredness is one thing, and burnout is another. There are times when we feel tired. We take a break, rejuvenate ourselves and get back to work. But burnout lasts longer, feels like an eternity and seems like being locked up in a dark and dingy place with little scope to see a ray of light. Put simply; burnout is a state of complete physical, mental and emotional drainage or exhaustion. Burnout can occur due to prolonged periods of stress, constant pressure and being trampled under an excessive workload. Though it may seem difficult to recover when you face burnout, it is not impossible to do so. A few steps that you can follow to recover from burnout gradually are:

  • ● Gain a Realistic Perspective on Self-Love
  • ● Recognise the Signs of Burnout
  • ● Address All the Symptoms
  • ● Recognise the Root Cause
  • ● Take Frequent Breaks
  • ● Do Not Push Your Limits
  • ● Log Off from Devices
  • ● Differentiate Between Hard Work and Excessive Labour
  • ● Practice Meditation
  • ● Do Things That Fuel Your Energy
  • ● Get Rid of Guilt

1. Recognise the Signs of Burnout:

The first step to recovering from a stage of burnout is acknowledging that you have exhausted all your resources and are undergoing the burnout phase. Take note of your physical, emotional and mental state and ascertain whether you are experiencing signs of burnout. Some signs of burnout include disinterestedness, no motivation, no energy, a pessimistic attitude, self-doubt, extreme exhaustion, purposelessness, and emotional instability.

2. Address All the Symptoms:

Once you have ascertained that you are undergoing a burnout phase, you need to start addressing the symptoms you experience. For instance, physical symptoms like headaches, body aches and fatigue need to be addressed by taking proper rest, nutrition and getting eight hours of sleep. Mental exhaustion and stress must be addressed by talking to a close friend or consulting a psychologist. Emotional instability and anxiety need to be addressed by practising meditation and breathing exercises.

3. Recognise the Root Cause:

Burnouts occur for a reason, and they must be detected. Solely working on addressing the symptoms of burnout without addressing the root cause will render all your efforts futile. So contemplate what caused you to experience burnout in the first place? Is it exam stress and pressure? Excessive school workload? Family problems? Or a combination of all these factors? Recognising the root cause behind your burnout will help you become more self-aware and let you gradually let go of the things that push you to a state of extreme exhaustion.

4. Take Frequent Breaks:

During burnout, one is already low on energy. So, working monotonously for prolonged hours will only worsen your exhaustion. Instead, when you need to work during a burnout phase, you must ensure that you get the work done without worsening your condition. So take frequent breaks between your work sessions. Keep a slot of the day all to yourself. For instance, you can keep the evenings or mornings completely free and devote time to yourself and your well-being.


5. Do Not Push Your Limits:

Enduring burnout almost feels like hanging on the edge of a cliff. Any extra pressure other than what you are already enduring will push you off the cliff and lead you to hit rock bottom in no time. And we surely don’t want that to happen. Pushing yourself to work/study more or challenging your limits is not a great idea during burnout. This is a time when you need to recover and be gentle with yourself.

6. Log Off from Devices:

Constantly being surrounded by laptops and smartphones that keep buzzing with text and email notifications can definitely add to piling stress and anxiety. You need to take some time off, log off from all devices around you, and spend some time in their absence. Disconnecting from devices for some time during the day will allow you to recharge from exhaustion and stress.

7. Differentiate Between Hard Work and Excessive Labour:

Most people fail to differentiate between hard work and excessive labour that works counterproductively and leads one to fall into the pit of shattering exhaustion. Working hard doesn’t imply overworking yourself to a point where you lose all your energy to work and collapse on your bed, not wanting to get up again. Anything in excess is detrimental to one’s well-being. Maintaining a balance between work/studies and rest or leisure is a necessity.

8. Practise Meditation:

Though you may feel extremely listless and lethargic to do anything during a burnout phase, you need to gather some energy to meditate. It might take a lot of willpower and determination to make up your mind to meditate when you are completely exhausted. But meditating will only work in your favour. Practising meditation and breathing exercises for just 15 minutes each day is all you need to provide your mind with some peace and restore your lost energy.

9. Do Things That Fuel Your Energy:

During your breaks, take some time to do things you enjoy doing. Avoid activities that require a lot of physical and mental effort and resort to simple activities like listening to music, watching a movie in bed, reading your favourite novel, doodling, sketching or anything you like. If all you want to do is sleep during your break, then go ahead and provide yourself with the rest you need.

10. Get Rid of Guilt:

Most importantly, understand that taking a rest or giving yourself a break is not a waste of time. Do not fall into the trap of obsessing over toxic productivity where you compromise your health and well-being for work and studies. Taking a break is productive as it helps you restore your energy and recover so that you can get back to work once you heal and give your best. So, please do not feel guilty for resting or taking a break when you need and deserve it.
