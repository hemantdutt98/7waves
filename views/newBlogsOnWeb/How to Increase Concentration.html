How to Increase Concentration?



Ananya K

Content Team, 7 Waves Life Solutions

June 27, 2022    10 min read

“The power of concentration is the only key to the treasure-house of knowledge.”

~Swami Vivekananda

Finally making up your mind to study, you sit on your desk with great determination to complete your studies only to find yourself scrolling through your phone a few minutes later. Maintaining concentration while studying is challenging, especially when distractions constantly surround us and our minds wander off to different thoughts. We are here to tell you that it’s not impossible to cultivate and develop concentration. You can improve your concentration abilities with practice and a little patience. Here are a few tips for you to follow:

  • ● Exclude Distractions
  • ● Write Down Your Tasks
  • ● Study in a Peaceful Environment
  • ● Read Novels/Stories
  • ● Plan a Time for Distractions
  • ● Practice Meditation
  • ● Take Rest
  • ● Engage in Mind-Stimulating Activities
  • ● Study for Short Intervals
  • ● Listen to Study Music


1. Exclude Distractions:

The most important hurdle that needs to be crossed to remain concentrated is to exclude all distractions from your surroundings. So, put your phone on silent or ‘Do Not Disturb’ and keep it away from your sight. A study conducted at the University of California revealed that people usually return to their original task 23 minutes after being disturbed by a notification. Moreover, most people check their phones every 47 seconds, which hampers their productivity immensely (GeekBox IT). Notifications, social media and friends’ texts can wait, but your time can’t.

2. Write Down Your Tasks:

When you write your tasks down on paper, you are more likely to get them done. Juggling with the thought of completing several tasks in your mind would only lead you to get distracted. Writing your tasks down in a ‘To-Do’ list would take this mental load off your mind enabling you to concentrate and complete your tasks.

3. Study in a Peaceful Environment:

The environment we study in has a significant impact on our concentration levels. Studying in a room amidst people and noise will be more distracting than studying in a calm and peaceful environment like a library. So, choose a spot in your home which is relatively quiet with little to no people around. This would allow you to concentrate better without getting disturbed by your surroundings.

4. Read Novels/Stories:

Most people complain about not being able to retain their concentration while reading. The best way to develop the habit of concentrating while reading is to read non-academic texts in your free time. You can start by reading short stories, poems or any genre of novel you like. Once you develop the ability to remain focused while reading non-academic texts, you will also use that ability to focus while reading during your study sessions.


5. Plan a Time for Distractions:

Though it is important to eliminate distractions while studying, it is nearly impossible to eliminate distractions from your schedule completely. Doing this would only make you crave to check your phone or scroll through social media all the more. Therefore, it is also better to schedule a time for your distractions. Plan a time in your schedule where you can reply to all of your friends’ texts, chat with them, check notifications and open social media apps. This would work as an incentive or reward after a productive study session and motivate you to concentrate while studying so that you can enjoy your ‘distraction’ time later.

6. Practice Meditation:

Meditation is a practice that helps one increase their ability to concentrate in the long run. Though you might not notice the benefits of meditating immediately, you will definitely feel a difference in your concentration levels and overall mental health in due course of time. You can start with simple pranayama, breathing exercises or even focus your attention on all five senses while meditating.

7. Take Rest:

Lack of sleep, exhaustion and a burnt-out state are often reasons behind one’s inability to concentrate. You can concentrate well on your studies only when you are physically, mentally and emotionally healthy. So, ensure to rest whenever you feel exhausted, get eight hours of sleep and even keep free days in your schedule where all you do is relax. This would rejuvenate your mind and body to function productively while studying.

8. Engage in Mind-Stimulating Activities:

For those of you who like playing games and engaging in activities, try your hands on performing activities that require some brain work. Try solving riddles, puzzles, sudoku or even playing chess. You can even play other number games or word games with your friends (ATLAS, for example). Activities that stimulate your brain and push it to think in innovative ways are a great way to enhance your concentration abilities.

9. Study for Short Intervals:

Studying for long hours would only be detrimental if you aim to improve your concentration. The key is to start with short study sessions and gradually increase your concentration over time. Start with 20-minute (or maximum 30-minute) study sessions where you solely focus on studying and think of nothing else. Take a 5 to 10-minute break after your study session gets over and start another cycle likewise. You can use a timer to help you keep track of the time.

10. Listen to Study Music:

Music is an instant mood-uplifting tool that calms your mind. However, study music is slightly different from the music you listen to during your break. It is essential to listen to soft and soothing music with no lyrics while studying. Heavy beats or lyrics might distract your mind, so it is better to avoid them while studying. You can find innumerable study music playlists on YouTube. Listening to soothing and relaxing music while studying will not only enhance your mood but will also help you focus on concentrating on your work.
