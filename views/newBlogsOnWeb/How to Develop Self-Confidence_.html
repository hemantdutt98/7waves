How To Develop Self-Confidence?



Ananya K

Content Team, 7 Waves Life Solutions

June 02, 2022    4 min read

"I'm bulletproof, nothing to lose.
Fire away! Fire Away!
You shoot me down, but I won't fall
I am titanium."
~David Guetta ft. Sia (Titanium)

Sia was so damn right when she sang these lyrics with all her might. It gave me chills! This song was my recluse when I needed an instant shot of motivation to boost my self-confidence. You can add this song to your motivation boosting playlist too :) On that note, I would like to say that there is no one, absolutely no one in this world, who has not doubted themselves at least once in their life. Seeping into thoughts of self-doubt and negative self-criticism bothers many people, including you and me. Breaking the shackles of negativity and marching toward the path of developing self-confidence is not impossible. You and I can do it together by simply shifting our perspectives, recognising our areas of improvement, accepting our shortcomings, journaling, networking, and facing our fears.



1. Change your Perspective

First, change your perspective from solely focusing your energy on negative and critical thoughts to more positive ones. We often fall into the vicious trap of constantly critiquing ourselves for what we lack. This leads us into further despair and gloom. Instead, your focus should be to recognise and appreciating your strengths and abilities. This would make you feel confident about the things you have command over. For instance, if you are good at oratory skills, then appreciate it and make utmost use of it.


2. Embrace your Flaws

"Be happy with being you. Love your flaws. Own your quirks. Ad know that you are just as perfect as anyone else, exactly as you are". ~Ariana Grande

Accepting your shortcomings forms the base of building your journey towards enhancing your self-confidence. No one is perfect, and that is perfectly okay. You are worthy and wonderful despite your flaws. So, accept your imperfections and don't burden yourself with a burdening pile of guilt for not being perfect in all aspects of your life.


3. Recognise Areas of Improvement

It is imperative that you recognise the aspects you wish to work on and improve on. Recognition and awareness are way more beneficial than locking yourself in a state of ignorance and denial. Say, for example, if you wish to improve your overall grades, recognise the subjects you need to specifically improve on and divert more of your energy and efforts into working hard on those subjects.


4. Journaling

A study conducted by Stice et al., 2006 revealed that the habit of journaling helps immensely in combatting depression among the youth (Holstee). Writing can open up an endless array of possibilities and paths. Write your feelings and emotions down in a journal regularly. Keeping a written record of your feelings will help you keep track of your moods and how you feel about yourself. Journaling is a very self-reflexive activity that will allow you to engage deeper with your thoughts and regulate your emotions.


5. Networking

"Alone we can do so little and together we can do so much" ~Helen Keller.

Solving problems and finding solutions becomes so much easier when done with people you trust and believe in. Whenever you feel incompetent and unsatisfied, talk to people who believe in you and see your potential. The idea is to build a healthy support system you can rely on. This system can include your teachers, friends, parents, siblings or anyone you trust. It's not the number of people that matters; it's more importantly about the quality of the company you keep.



6. Face your fears

know this sounds intimidating and fear-inducing, but we need to overcome our challenges at some point, don't we? Working towards confronting your fears or failures is the best way to gain control and power in your hands. If you are afraid of the exam season, then the best way to come out of this is to tell yourself that you have nothing to lose. It's just a learning lesson. Prepare for the exam, go to the exam hall with confidence, and work on eliminating your exam fear. This job requires time, practice and patience, but it's worth the effort!

credits: Work Cited Holstee. “Benefits of Journaling for Mental Health: 5 Science-Based Journaling Benefits.” Holstee, 28 May 2022, www.holstee.com/blogs/mindful-matter/5-science-based-benefits-of-journaling
