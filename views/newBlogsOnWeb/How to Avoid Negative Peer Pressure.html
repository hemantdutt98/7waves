How to Avoid Negative Peer Pressure?



Ananya K

Content Team, 7 Waves Life Solutions

June 29, 2022    10 min read

Good friends make our lives so much better, don’t they? We look forward to going to school just so we can meet them and talk our hearts out. The people who genuinely consider you their friend and vice versa will cherish you for who you are and take you towards a better life along with them. But what about those instances when the people you thought of as friends don’t seem like friends anymore. When they push you to do things that you don’t like. When you feel uncomfortable in their company. You must acknowledge the influence and impact that your company has on you and be firm to avoid negative peer pressure. On that note, we have curated a few tips that will help you in avoiding negative peer pressure:

  • ● Gain a Realistic Perspective on Self-Love
  • ● Trust your Conviction
  • ● Don’t Consent to Things That Seem Uncomfortable t
  • ● Stick to Your Morals and Principles
  • ● Don’t Feel Pressured to Fit in and Impress
  • ● Say “No” When Required
  • ● Take Your Stand
  • ● Plan a Few Excuses
  • ● Create a Gradual Distance
  • ● Confront Your Peer Group If Needed
  • ● Talk to Someone Trustworthy
  • ● Interact with More People

1. Trust your Conviction:

Listen to your inner voice and pay attention to what it says. Understand how your friend circle makes you feel and how they treat you. Analyse if they are your true friends or not. If you feel something is wrong, then it is. And you should get yourself out of a toxic and unhealthy friend circle.

2. Don’t Consent to Things That Seem Uncomfortable:

Peer pressure takes a highly negative form when it pushes you to do things you are uncomfortable with. Ensure that you don’t let yourself give in to peer pressure and do something you don’t like. So don’t consent to do things that don’t seem right to you.

3. Stick to Your Morals and Principles:

Be true to your conscience and analyse if your friends are right or wrong. Don’t compromise on your morals to fulfil your friends’ demands. Be it consuming alcohol, drugs, smoking or anything else. Avoid doing anything that stands against your conscience and morals.

4. Don’t Feel Pressured to Fit in and Impress:

Remember, you are not obligated to impress your friend circle, match up to their standard of being “cool”, or seek approval from them. Don’t push yourself to do things that are not your cup of tea or become someone you are not solely because you want to fit and impress.


5. Say “No” When Required:

It takes courage, and not everyone is comfortable with saying an upfront “No”. But if the situation requires and your friend circle’s behaviour impacts you adversely, you need to be firm in saying “No”. Conveying the fact that you are not okay with their behaviour and approach is a crucial step in ensuring that they don’t continue with the same behaviour.

6. Take Your Stand:

Distinguish your opinions from that of your friend circle. Don’t take a decision in favour of your friends’ opinions if it’s detrimental to you and your wellness. Take your stand, stick to your decisions, and choose what you feel is right regardless of how much your peer circle pressurises you to give in.

7. Plan a Few Excuses:

When you have recognised the pattern of your peer circle’s behaviour and understood the things they pressurise you to do, plan a few excuses beforehand to save yourself from those situations. For instance, if you know that your peers usually force you to splurge money on outings or consume alcohol, you can keep a few excuses ready beforehand. Such as– you have other commitments or classes, or you’re not feeling well, so you won’t be able to join them.

8. Create a Gradual Distance:

When your peer circle becomes a source of stress and discomfort, it’s high time for you to keep a safe distance from them and stay away from their company. The best way to do this is to gradually distance yourself from them over time. You can start by spending a little less time with them, interacting less and responding less to their demands.

9. Confront Your Peer Group If Needed:

If your peer circle’s actions and behaviour towards you are intolerable and take more severe forms (like bullying), impacting your mental health, confront them immediately. Tell them you are not okay with their behaviour and snap ties with them. It’s better to end toxic relations than to suffer while surviving amidst them.

10. Talk to Someone Trustworthy:

Keeping a trustworthy and reliable person informed of your situation is essential so that someone has your back in case things go out of hand. Whether it’s your school teachers, parents, a close friend or a relative- pick someone you trust and tell them about your circumstances. Take their advice and share your feelings with them. Getting out of an unhealthy peer circle will be easier when you have someone to support you.

11. Interact with More People:

Just because one friend circle turned out to be detrimental for you doesn’t necessarily mean that all peer circles would impact you the same way. Explore your options and interact with new people and befriend those who share the same interests as you. Be in the company of like-minded people who support you and accept you for who you are.
